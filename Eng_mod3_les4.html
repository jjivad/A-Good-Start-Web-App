Module 3: Money Matters- Introduction to Financial Literacy

Lesson 4- Prioritizing what you Buy

Script

In the last lesson, we explored our personal values and how they impact our wants and needs (choices). Today we are going to take that a step further by prioritizing what we spend our money on.

Let us start by reflecting on the meaning of the proverb, Roadside items are nice, but it takes money to buy. Think about it and we’ll come back at it at the end of the lesson.

What is prioritizing?

Prioritizing means understanding that something is more important than something else. To prioritize something means to put it first.

For Example: Choosing to go to bed early instead of staying up late, so that you can be up for football practice in the morning, I am prioritizing going to bed early over hanging out with my friends because soccer practice is important to me.

How do we prioritize things to buy?

It’s as easy as 1-2-3. Let’s look at the 1-2-3 system to help us explore our priorities and what is important for our health and wellbeing.

1 are Items that are essential for healthy living - These are our NEEDS

2 are Items that are not essential but important to us - These could be NEEDS depending on our values.

3 are Items that are not essential and not important - These are our WANTS

Now that we know what is meant by prioritizing our needs, let’s look at our list of items from last session which include: Water, a car, food, clothing, a bike, football, shelter, a computer, a phone and health care. Find a partner, decide out of the needs, which are the most important and which are the least important. You will rank the needs in order of importance, using the 1-2-3 system and explain to each other why you have chosen the items in each.

Going back to the proverb we mentioned at the beginning of the lesson, Roadside items are nice, but it takes money to buy.
This proverb reminds us that although things may be nice, they are not always a priority. We must first prioritize the things that will meet our survival needs, before buying our wants.

What a great class looking at priorities! I hope you have learnt a lot about trying to figure out what is most important to you and how that will contribute to your health and wellbeing. Over the next few days, think more about the things that are important to you.

Thank you for listening. This is the end of lesson 4!
