Module 1 : Personal Motivations, Goals and Transformation

Lesson 4- Turning Challenge into Opportunity: Using Positive Thinking

Script

Now that we have discussed who we are and what our motivations are, our next step is to see how we can reframe our perspective. How do WE take our challenges? How do WE turn our challenges into opportunities? Today we will focus on taking OUR challenges and fears and turning them into inspiration and opportunities.

We will begin with two African proverbs, reflect on them, try and get their similarity and we will come back to them at the end of the lesson.

Ist Proverb: When a home is burnt down, the rebuilt home is more beautiful
2nd proverb: The best time to plant a tree is 20 years ago; the second-best time is now.

First, let us understand what an opportunity is.
An opportunity is a time or set of circumstances that makes it possible to do something. The key word here is POSSIBILITY!

In this lesson, we will focus on turning our challenges into possibilities with positive thinking.
Let us answer the following questions: You can write down your answers or share with a friend

  1. If you choose any one item that you would never run out of, what would it be and why?
  2. If you could be an animal, what animal would you be and why?
  3. If you could choose an age that you could be forever, what age would you choose and why?

Now let us look at our challenges. What are some of the challenges you face in your daily life?

Examples might be: Challenges to getting food, Challenges getting a job, Challenges accessing formal education and many more.

Let’s see how we can turn the problems/challenges into OPPORTUNITIES through the concept of POSSIBILITY!

Think about how we use words/ideas/thinking to create meaning in our lives. How we think/describe things has so much impact on how we see them… for example, look at the two proverbs from the beginning of class.

The first proverb, when a home is burnt down, the rebuilt home is more beautiful, means, although the house is burnt down, instead of focusing on the burnt house- we focus on rebuilding a more beautiful house! – We are taking a challenge/problem and turning it into an opportunity.

Take a few moments and identify how the challenge is turned into an opportunity in the second proverb.

Now that we have looked at the language of possibility in the proverbs, how do we apply this to the challenges we face? This does not mean we will find an instant solution to our challenges, No. but we will find a different way of looking at them!

For example, we can restate the challenges using questions. A challenge such, as not having enough food to eat, we can turn that to a possibility by asking, “In what ways might we work together to ensure we are all fed?”

Now, think about your challenges and come up with other ways to change them into a possibility.

Has this made you see challenges differently? Using language to turn challenges to possibilities gives us a feeling of hope, happiness, positivity, inspiration and possibility.

Using the language of possibility allows us to take our challenges, reframe them and turn them into possibilities. Over the next few days, as you face challenges in your life, think about ways that you can reframe them into possibilities!

Thank you for listening, this is the end of lesson 4!
