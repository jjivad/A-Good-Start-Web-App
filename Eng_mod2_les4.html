Lesson 4: How do we solve problems? - Problem Solving Cycle

In the last session, we talked a lot about emotions and how people feel in difficult situations.
Can anyone remember any of the feelings/emotions that we talked about last seminar?

Today, we are going to focus on finding good ways to solve problems so that we can get the outcomes/opportunities that we want in our lives.

To start off, what do you think is the meaning of the African proverb: Strategy is better than strength? Think about it and we’ll come to it later.

Now, let us look at the problem solving process: When we want to achieve our goals and dreams, we often have to solve problems to overcome our challenges.

Did you know that there are special steps to make problem solving easier? Let’s look at them now.

First step: Identify the problem - make sure that you know what the problem is.

Second step: Understand how everyone feels/what they want - Understanding how everyone is feeling helps us to find solutions.

Third step: Identify possible solutions - think of ways to improve the situation by taking into account everyone’s feelings

Fourth step: Evaluate the possible solutions - what are the results/outcomes of each solution?

Fifth step: Select a solution - choose the solution that seems to make the most sense and is most relevant for your problem.

Back to the proverb we mentioned earlier, Strategy is better than strength. The proverb means, with strategy you can win a battle without fighting, but with strength you have to fight in order to win.

Now that we have gone through the problem solving steps, I want you to share with another person something new that you have learned in this lesson. It can be something simple or complex- just one thing you feel was a new idea.

We have come to the end of lesson 4, thank you for listening!
