Module 5: What's My Worth?
Exploring My Personal and Sexual Well-being

Lesson 2: Dealing with Pressure from peers or friends

Script

Today, I want us to discuss ways in which we can deal with pressure from other people in a positive way to enable us to have the right frame of mind within our relationships.

What do you believe is the meaning of the saying, A dead fish shall always follow the current of a river?
Think about it and weâ€™ll come back to it at the end of the lesson.

Let us explore what it means to have a friend or partner influence you to do something that you are not comfortable with.

Find a friend with whom you can discuss these questions:

  1. Have you ever been influenced by your friends or partners to do things that you did not feel comfortable with? In that situation, what would you have done differently? (Pause)
  2. In your sexual and personal relationships, are you sometimes influenced to do things or engage in practices that you do not feel comfortable with? (Pause)
  3. In your family relationships, are you influenced to behave in a certain way or do certain things because your family expects it and not because you want to? (Pause)
  4. How has culture affected how you behave in your relationships? (Pause)
  5. Has social media and free access to information, influenced how you view your relationships? Your sexual encounters? Your cultural heritage? (Pause)

Now, I want you to write a letter to your younger self. If you want, you can just make up a speech in your head for yourself instead. Tell that younger self what you wish someone would have told you now.

Back to the proverb we mentioned at the beginning of the session, A dead fish shall always follow the current of a river.

This saying states that a river has a current that always flows in a certain direction. Fish that are alive can swim against the current and they do not always follow it. However, a fish that is dead cannot swim but only goes with the current, and therefore someone who has no character always goes with the crowd, never thinking or analyzing what is best for them, but doing things to only please others.

This session was very heavy with a lot of conversations. Through it all I hope that you have been able to think about the various ways in which you can manage peer pressure.

Thank you for listening. This is the end of lesson 2!
