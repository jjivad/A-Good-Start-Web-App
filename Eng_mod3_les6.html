Module 3: Money Matters- Introduction to Financial Literacy

Lesson 6: Income and Budgeting

Script

Now that we have explored the role of budgeting in our lives, let’s take a look at income and how it relates to budgeting.

Let’s begin by looking at 2 proverbs

1st proverb: A single bracelet does not jingle.
2nd proverb: A single stick may smoke, but it will not burn.

What do you think the two proverbs mean?
Think about it and we’ll get back to it at the end of the lesson.

What do you think the word income means?

Income is money received through work and stipends

To understand this better, add up the TOTAL income for this family. You can write the numbers down or do it with a friend.

In a family, Momma makes 2350 Ksh , Papa makes 1300 Ksh, Yonas makes 400 Ksh, Liya makes 1400 Ksh , Kamali and Ife don’t make anything because they are both still babies.

How much does this family of 6 make in total? (Pause)

Now let’s explore budgeting this 5450 Ksh by exploring the family’s expenses.

For a month they have the following expenses:

  • Baby formula: 1300 Ksh,
  • Food: 2500 Ksh,
  • School fees: 500 Ksh,
  • Diapers: 400 Ksh,
  • Soap: 200 Ksh
    • (Pause)

Now discuss with your friend the following questions;

  1. Are they able to make enough money to cover their needs?
  2. Do they have any money left for wants?
  3. How much money do they have left?
  4. What should they do with the money they have left?

Let’s revisit the proverbs we mentioned at the beginning of the lesson.

1st proverb: A single bracelet does not jingle.
2nd proverb: A single stick may smoke, but it will not burn.

These proverbs mean, we are more successful when we pool our income or work together toward a common goal. So, when we put our money together, we can get more done.

I trust the module had a great impact to how you view your needs and wants, money and how to budget.

Thank you for listening. This is the end of lesson 6 and of Module 3!
