Lesson 4: What is my worth?

Script

Today we shall explore our self-worth.

We will begin by reflecting on the following proverbs

1st proverb: People learn how to treat you based on what you accept from them
2nd proverb: Sometimes the hardest part of the journey is believing that you are worthy of the trip

Think about their meaning and weâ€™ll get back to that at the end of the lesson.

Now, let us look at our self-worth. It is important to have a great value of oneself, before you can expect others to respect and value you as well.

Find a partner and discuss the following questions, or just think about them on your own:

  • How is your self- worth related to your ability to be in healthy sexual relationships?
  • Can there be too much when it comes to exploring your sexuality?
  • What do you think of transactional sex? Sex in trade for gifts or money
  • Is it easier for men to pay for sex than women and why?
  • Are men able to be more sexually permissive than women? Why?
  • What is more important, how other people value you, or how you value yourself

1st proverb: People learn how to treat you based on what you accept from them
People treat you how you let them treat you.

2nd proverb: Sometimes the hardest part of the journey is believing that you are worthy of the trip
Explanation: Self-doubt can be your own worst enemy. Believe in yourself- believe that you are worthy of love and respect.

Thank you for listening. We come to the end of lesson 4!
